Design System · Styleguide

Monochrome on purpose.

An editorial sports-franchise platform. Near-black canvas, white Helvetica at weight 500 with tight negative tracking. A strict split — admin chrome stays monochrome; saturated Spectrum color and the house gradients live only on atmospheric surfaces. The product is monochrome so that people are what's in color when they show up.

06

The dot pill — status without a glyph

The editorial answer to a status icon. The dot is the only colored element; pill background and border stay monochrome, the text never takes a spec color and never goes all-caps. One color per pill. The dot signals; the word names.

On ne vend pas des sièges. On vend des soirées.

Exactly one oversized manifesto per page. Full-bleed gradient overriding the 1280px cap, white type verified for AAA contrast over the darker side of the gradient. This is the rhythm device that prevents the page from reading as "tech."

09

Photography & mockups

Photography carries the warmth the chrome refuses. The image is never colored — no duotone, no grayscale, no overlay. Only a colorless film-grain texture (~6%) touches it. For color + photo, layer a solid block on top.

[ fan portrait · documentary color · grain ~6% ]
Color combines through a solid block — never an overlay.
Subject hierarchy. Faces mid-emotion → crowds → stadium architecture → operational moments.
Documentary color. Slightly desaturated, film-not-Instagram. Full-color, untouched.
Solid blocks, not washes. The block carries the color; the image stays clean beneath it.
Pairing rule. Every mockup ships with a human moment in the same section. The mockup proves the software exists; the photo proves who it's for.
Forbidden. Laptops showing the product, conference rooms, handshakes, stock headshots, lens flares, empty seats in marketing.
