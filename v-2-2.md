# Stadli

## Overview
Stadli feels like a cinematic, creative and immersive aura minimal matchnite where calm & bold meet. The interface is intentionally minimal, dark, and centered, with strong editorial hero treatments that makes the product feel powerful, cinematic, documentary, and aspirational. It targets teams and staff who want a tool to fill stadiums and get creative.

## Colors
- **Primary (#FFFFFF):** Crisp white used for the main call-to-action, key text, and high-contrast highlights. It carries the strongest visual weight against the black interface.
- **Secondary (#262626):** A charcoal gray used for secondary buttons and elevated controls. It softens the hierarchy without breaking the dark mood.
- **Tertiary (#404040):** A slightly lighter gray for links, subtle text, and low-emphasis UI. It supports the interface without competing with the primary content.
- **Neutral (#000000):** Deep black forms the overall canvas and gives the site its cinematic, immersive atmosphere.
- **Surface (#0A0A0A):** A near-black surface tone for panels and inputs when separation from the page background is needed while staying tonal.
- **On-surface (#FFFFFF):** White text and icon color used on dark surfaces for clarity and accessibility.
- **Border (#E5E5E5):** A light border tone used sparingly in cards and controls where a crisp edge is needed.
- **Muted (#B3B3B3):** Soft gray for supporting copy and low-priority labels.
- **Error (#FF5A5F):** A vivid red reserved for destructive actions and error states; it should remain rare so the palette stays restrained.

## Typography
The system uses Suisse Intl across headings, body, and labels, which gives the experience a clean, modern, slightly editorial character. Headlines are light in weight at 400, large in scale, and tightly controlled in line height for a polished hero-first presentation. Body text stays highly legible at 16px/24px, while labels and button text use a slightly denser 14px treatment with a book-like weight around 450 to feel refined rather than bold. There is no visible uppercase or wide-tracking convention; typography is understated and relies on size, spacing, and contrast instead of ornament.

## Layout & Spacing
The page uses a centered, fixed hero composition with generous negative space around the main message and product visual. Navigation is stretched horizontally across the top, while the hero content is anchored in the middle, creating a strong axis and premium storefront feel. Spacing follows a restrained rhythm based on 6px, 14px, 24px, 48px, and 96px steps, with larger section gaps and compact internal control spacing. Cards and embedded panels typically use 24px padding, while primary controls keep a tight 8px/16px button rhythm.

## Elevation & Depth
Depth is created mostly through contrast, blur, and atmospheric layering rather than visible UI chrome. The system is effectively flat in its navigation and text treatments, but the hero images uses strong contrast and effects/lights/leaks/gradients/blurs/noise/motion and soft glow to separate the product mockups from the black background. When surfaces do need structure, they rely on subtle borders and tonal differences instead of heavy shadows.

## Shapes
The shape language is soft and rounded, especially for interactive elements. Buttons use a full pill radius, while cards and panels use a 16px to 24px range for a more composed, architectural feel. Overall, the system balances futuristic softness with clean precision.

## Components
Buttons are the most expressive component in the system. `button-primary` is a white pill with black text, used for the main conversion action and sized at 36px tall with compact horizontal padding. `button-secondary` uses charcoal fill with white text and the same pill shape for secondary actions like launching the app. `button-tertiary` is visually minimal and should be used for low-emphasis links or subtle actions.

Cards should feel cinematic and clean when used as foreground containers, with image backgrounds and ui-elements layered when needed, very subtle borders, and 24px padding. Keep them simple and avoid decorative shadows unless a component needs separation from a busy background. Inputs should follow the same restrained logic: dark or near-black surfaces, soft borders, rounded corners, and white text for readability.

Cards definition: A cinematic ui card that combines UI screenshots, photography, floating micro-panels and storytelling into one premium visual scene. It acts less like a literal screenshot and more like a storytelling component that quickly communicates workflow, creativity, product value, scale, and trust.

Chips and small pills should mirror the button language with full rounding and compact padding. Use them for filters, tags, or quick actions, and keep their color treatment subdued so they do not compete with the main CTA. Navigation items should remain lightweight and text-driven, with only the active or primary action receiving button styling.

Charts look like posters: the title says the takeaway, not just the axis. Bars start from zero, lines stay flat and clean — no gradients, no shadows, no glow. Almost everything is black-and-white, with a single colored line or dot carrying the story. Extra emphasis comes from shape, not more color: dashed lines for forecasts, one highlighted dot for an outlier, a heavier stroke for the line that matters. Colors are limited to green, red, orange, and blue, each with a fixed meaning. Numbers use even-width figures, and every point sits on a near-black background.

## Do's and Don'ts
- Do keep the page dark, sparse, and centered so the hero remains the focus.
- Do use white for the most important action and highest-contrast text.
- Do keep buttons pill-shaped and compact, with restrained padding.
- Do use Suisse Intl consistently for all text styles.
- Don't introduce bright accent colors that break the monochrome mood.
- Don't add heavy shadows, loud gradients, or glossy effects to standard UI controls.
- Don't make secondary actions as visually prominent as the primary CTA.
- Don't tighten the layout so much that the composition loses its spacious, cinematic feel.
