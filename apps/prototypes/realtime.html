Brand & guidance

Aurora

Aurora is a stark, fully monochrome system: a pure-white canvas, a neutral graphite ink ramp, and a near-black accent (#1a1a1a) reserved for primary actions and focus. There is no chromatic colour at all — hierarchy comes entirely from contrast, weight, and whitespace, with hairline borders separating surfaces rather than shadow. Display type is set tight and dark; body copy relaxes for reading. The mood is precise and editorial: quiet, high-contrast, and typographic. A single brand hue can be introduced later, but the baseline is intentionally black-and-white.

Overview

Aurora is a stark, fully monochrome surface. The thesis is disciplined contrast: a white canvas, a neutral ink ramp, and a near-black accent for actions — no chromatic colour at all. The single thing to get right is contrast discipline; hierarchy must come from weight, size, and space, never hue.

Colors

Brand & Accent

The accent is near-black ink (#1a1a1a), used only on primary buttons, focus rings, and the brand mark. There is no chromatic colour in the base system — introduce at most one brand hue later, and only for actions.

Surface

Surfaces step quietly: canvas for the page, surface-1 for standard cards, surface-2 for featured or nested panels, surface-3 for wells and insets. Levels are separated by hairline borders rather than shadow.

Text

Ink runs strongest to faintest — ink for headings and high-emphasis copy, ink-muted for body, ink-subtle for captions and metadata, ink-tertiary for placeholders and disabled labels.

Semantic

Success, error, and warning are rendered as neutral greys so the base system stays fully monochrome. If you later add a brand colour, reserve saturated hues for these states only — never for decoration.

Typography

Font Family

Inter carries everything — display and body — with JetBrains Mono reserved for code, token values, and technical captions. There is no third face.

Hierarchy

display-xl is the hero headline; display-lg / display-md open major sections; headline and card-title label sub-sections and cards; body and body-lg carry prose; the uppercase eyebrow tags sections; caption handles metadata.

Principles

Display sizes tighten with negative tracking (down to -2px); body relaxes to 400 / 1.6 for readability. Sentence case everywhere except the uppercase eyebrow. Weight and size — not colour — create emphasis.

Note on Font Substitutes

If Inter is unavailable, fall back to system-ui / -apple-system; for the mono face use ui-monospace / SF Mono. The substitute should keep a neutral geometric-humanist tone and a similar x-height.

Layout

Spacing System

Spacing follows an 8px-based scale (2 / 4 / 8 / 16 / 24 / 40 / 64) with a 96px section rhythm. Related elements sit at the small end; sections breathe with xl–section gaps.

Grid & Container

Content is capped at a 1120px container centered within a 24px gutter, on a 12-column grid. Long-form reading narrows to ~760px; the 1320px wide track is reserved for full-bleed media.

Whitespace Philosophy

Airy but not sparse. Generous vertical rhythm between sections; hairlines and whitespace — not boxes — do the separating.

Elevation & Depth

Mostly flat. Depth is signalled by surface tone and hairline borders, not drop shadows. Elevation is reserved for transient overlays such as menus, popovers, and modals.

Decorative Depth

Decoration is minimal: no gradients or glows in the core UI. At most a single soft accent wash behind the hero, used sparingly if at all.

Shapes

Border Radius Scale

Radii are gentle: sm–md (4–8px) on inputs and small controls, lg (12px) on cards, xl on large media panels, pill on buttons and tags. The feel is soft-but-precise.

Photography & Illustration Geometry

Imagery is framed in rounded (lg) containers, cropped to 16:9 or 16:8 for heroes and 1:1 for avatars and logos. Clean, product-forward, low-noise.

Components

Buttons

Three tiers: primary (solid near-black ink fill, pill), secondary (canvas with hairline-strong border), tertiary (text-only, ink-muted). Hover deepens to black; focus shows a grey ring; disabled drops to ~45% opacity.

Cards & Containers

Cards are surface-1 panels with a hairline border, lg radius, and lg padding — no shadow. Feature cards step up to surface-2 to draw the eye without adding colour.

Inputs & Forms

Fields sit on canvas with a hairline-strong border and md radius. Focus swaps the border to primary-focus with a soft grey ring. Labels are body-sm, sentence case; validation errors use the semantic-error tone inline.

Navigation

A 64px sticky top nav on canvas with a hairline bottom border: mark and wordmark left, links center-left, sign-in (tertiary) and the primary CTA right. On mobile the links collapse behind a menu; the CTA stays visible.

Signature Components

The signature is the restraint itself: hairline-bordered cards on a pure-white canvas, driven entirely by a neutral ink ramp with no colour. If one component defines the brand, it is the solid black pill button.

Do's and Don'ts

Do

  • Build hierarchy from contrast, weight, and space — not colour.
  • Separate sections with hairlines and whitespace, not boxes.
  • Lead sections with an uppercase eyebrow and a display title.
  • Keep the system fully monochrome unless a brand hue is explicitly needed.

Don't

  • Don't add drop shadows to flat cards.
  • Don't introduce colour without a deliberate reason.
  • Don't rely on tone alone where weight or size would read better.
  • Don't mix more than one display weight within a section.

Responsive Behavior

Breakpoints

Three intents: mobile (<640px, single column), tablet (640–1024px, two columns), desktop (>1024px, the full 12-column grid at the 1120px container).

Touch Targets

Interactive targets are at least 44×44px, with a minimum of 8px spacing between adjacent controls.

Collapsing Strategy

Multi-column grids stack to one column, nav links collapse to a menu, and side-by-side media and text reflow to stacked. The primary CTA never hides.

Image Behavior

Images scale fluidly within their rounded frames, keeping their crop ratio; heroes may switch to a tighter crop on mobile. Logos never stretch.

Iteration Guide

To extend the system, add a token to the relevant group — it flows automatically to the live :root, the preview, and the export — then document the decision here. Preserve the monochrome, contrast-first and hairline-structure rules when adding components, colours, or page types.

Known Gaps

Not yet covered: dark-mode surface mapping, a data-visualisation palette, motion and transition tokens, and full iconography guidance.
